Wisdom from Industry Leaders

Inspirational quotes to motivate your career journey

"Your work is going to fill a large part of your life, and the only way to be truly satisfied is to do what you believe is great work. And the only way to do great work is to love what you do. If you haven't found it yet, keep looking. Don't settle."

Steve Jobs

Co-founder of Apple Inc.

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was an American entrepreneur and business magnate who co-founded Apple Computer. His vision for personal computing revolutionized multiple industries including computers, smartphones, music, and animation.
"When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor. Failure is an option here. If things are not failing, you are not innovating enough."

Elon Musk

CEO of Tesla and SpaceX

Elon Musk is a technology entrepreneur and investor who has founded or co-founded several companies including Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, and The Boring Company. He is known for his ambitious vision of the future spanning electric vehicles, space exploration, and artificial intelligence.
"If you're offered a seat on a rocket ship, don't ask what seat! Just get on. Career progression is not a ladder anymore; it's more like a jungle gym. Look for opportunities, take risks, and don't be afraid to pivot."

Sheryl Sandberg

COO of Facebook (Meta)

Sheryl Sandberg is an American technology executive, activist, and author. She served as the chief operating officer of Facebook (now Meta) and is the founder of LeanIn.Org. Her book "Lean In" sparked global conversations about women in the workplace.
"If you decide that you're going to do only the things you know are going to work, you're going to leave a lot of opportunity on the table. What really matters is, companies that don't continue to experiment, companies that don't embrace failure, they eventually get in a desperate position where the only thing they can do is a Hail Mary bet at the very end of their corporate existence."

Jeff Bezos

Founder of Amazon

Jeff Bezos is an American entrepreneur who founded Amazon in 1994 as an online bookstore and grew it into the world's largest online retailer. His leadership transformed e-commerce and cloud computing through Amazon Web Services.
